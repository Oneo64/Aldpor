All names below, unless specified, are the Daggsvaldian name for it.

Planes/Realms:
	Talygva     = Material realm

	Jælhleid     = Good afterlife (LG, paradise realm to good people)
	Hel         = Bad afterlife (CG, punishment realm to evil people)

	Furnalfa    = Realm of Fey and Pure Nature (N, infinite dark lush green forest)
	Sotvaryg    = Realm of Earth and Stone

Dimensions:
	Vorasil     = Ethereal plane
	Maresil     = Astral plane
	Parhrüvaryg = Plane of Magic (appears to be a sea of a purple sparkly type of liquid, commonly known as "Parhrügi's Tears". Parhrügi's Tears are extremely volatile, and a single vial can be worth tens of gold)

Deities:
	Talmundr:
		Domain:      God of Nature, Tempest, and Luck
		Symbol:      Dagsvaldian 'T' rune, upside down
		Other names: Talûsa (Aodesvu), Tereven (Hendril), Tànûssiel (Rànûdel), Tadhuundar (Banod, Glomdur), Tsakasten (Mornesvid)

	Lünanyr:
		Domain:      God of Life, Death, and Nature
		Symbol:      4 pointed star
		Other names: Lûnania (Aodesvu), Lunara (Hendril), Lomathelis (Rànûdel), Luunakir (Banod, Glomdur), Lorazin (Mornesvid)

	Jalfar:
		Domain:      God of Creation and Destruction
		Symbol:      An eye
		Other names: Yulvis (Aodesvu), Yorgarar (Hendril), Yiisessil (Rànûdel), Yorvar (Banod, Glomdur), Edorfil (Mornesvid)

	Parhrügi:
		Domain:      God of Magic
		Symbol:      A circle surrounded by 8 smaller circles (each small circle symbolizes a school of magic)
		Other names: Pöldànis (Aodesvu), Parletha (Hendril), Pönanis (Rànûdel), Padhuuki (Banod, Glomdur), Plakisael (Mornesvid)

	Hirulfyr:
		Domain:      God of Emotions
		Symbol:      A drop of water
		Other names: Himarir (Aodesvu), Hirnest (Hendril), Ainassare (Rànûdel), Hiiruldir (Banod, Glomdur), Hegetsen (Mornesvid)
